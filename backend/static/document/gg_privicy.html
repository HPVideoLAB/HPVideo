What data we collect

During operation, HPVideo may collect performance data and system information to help us identify issues. Some of this information may be sent to us in crash reports if you've opted in to this feature in your device or operating system settings.

HPVideo will store a portion of user and large model historical conversation information, which allows users to quickly retrieve key information. Of course, users can choose to delete this information. The system will automatically delete user data within 90 days.

What data we share

We may share crash reports, full or partial logs, and other application reliability data with other projects, teams, organizations, or companies as required to debug issues in HPVideo applications.

Firebase usage and data collection

In addition to the data mentioned above, HPVideo uses Firebase, a service provided by Google, to improve the stability and performance of our applications. Firebase collects data that may include, but is not limited to, the following:

  • Device Information: This may include device type, operating system version, and unique device identifiers such as Advertising ID or Android ID.
  • Application Usage: This includes events such as application installs, updates, and usage frequency, as well as user engagement metrics.
  • Crash Reports: Firebase Crashlytics automatically collects crash reports to help us identify and resolve stability issues in the application.
  • Performance Data: Firebase Performance Monitoring helps us understand the performance of our app in the hands of users by collecting information on app speed, responsiveness, and resource usage.

Firebase may automatically collect this data to provide us with insights that allow us to improve the app, even if you do not manually report any issues.

Opting Out of Firebase Analytics

If you wish to opt out of Firebase Analytics tracking, you can do so by disabling tracking in your device settings. For more information on how Firebase collects and processes data, please refer to Google's Privacy Policy and Firebase's Privacy and Security documentation.
